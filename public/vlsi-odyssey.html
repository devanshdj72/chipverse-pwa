December 23, 1947. Bell Laboratories, Murray Hill, New Jersey. John Bardeen, Walter Brattain, and William Shockley demonstrated something almost impossibly small — a device that could amplify signals using semiconducting crystals. A transistor.

WAFER GROWTH

Pure silicon crystal grown from molten silicon. 300mm diameter ingot sliced to 0.7mm wafers

OXIDATION

Thermal SiO₂ gate dielectric grown. Sub-nm precision. Electrical insulation between gate and channel

03

DEPOSITION

CVD/PVD layers. Polysilicon, metals, dielectrics. Atomic layer deposition for ultimate precision

EUV LITHOGRAPHY

05

ETCHING

Plasma removes material with atomic precision. Dry etch or wet etch, sub-Angstrom control

06

ION IMPLANT

Dopant atoms fired at wafer surface. Precise control of semiconductor properties

07

CMP

Chemical-Mechanical Planarization — polishes wafer flat to atomic smoothness between layers

08

METALLIZATION

Copper interconnects deposited and patterned. 15+ metal layers in modern chips
